The Key Nine Studios interface is designed to keep navigation predictable: primary work happens in the center canvas, global navigation lives on the left sidebar, and contextual actions appear in the inspector on the right. Spend a few minutes learning these three regions and you will move between them fluidly.

## Interface regions

### Sidebar

The sidebar contains entry points for projects, automation, and your account. Pin frequently visited spaces if your client supports it so daily destinations stay one click away. On smaller screens the sidebar collapses—use the menu control to reopen it without losing your place on the canvas.

### Canvas

The canvas is the main surface for viewing and editing work. It adapts to the content type: document pages, project boards, and workflow builders each render in the canvas.

### Inspector

The inspector shows metadata and contextual actions for whatever you have selected. An empty inspector usually means nothing is selected yet, not that you lack permissions.

<Note>
Some workflows open transient panels—comments, version history, AI suggestions. Closing a panel returns focus to the canvas. Keyboard users can tab through interactive regions in DOM order.
</Note>

## Command palette

Open the command palette with **⌘K** (Mac) or **Ctrl+K** (Windows/Linux) to jump anywhere in the product by name. The palette respects your permissions—destinations you cannot access either do not appear or show as disabled with a reason.

### Search vs navigate

Use the command palette for fuzzy jumps when you know a name fragment. Use global search (⌘P / Ctrl+P) for full-text results across content bodies and attachments.

## Settings

Workspace settings live under your avatar in the bottom-left corner. Project settings are scoped per project and found in the project's sidebar menu.

<Tip>
When in doubt about which settings layer you are in, check the breadcrumb or page title. You do not want to change org-wide SSO when you meant to tweak a single project's webhook configuration.
</Tip>
