Projects in Key Nine Studios are the boundary for permissions, notifications, and automation. Spending two minutes on naming and visibility settings up front prevents accidental content exposure later—especially when customers or partners are invited as collaborators.

## Create a new project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click New project">
    From the sidebar or your dashboard, click **New project**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the project">
    Use a name people will recognize in notifications and URLs. Avoid confidential codenames in public-facing titles—put internal codenames in a separate description field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set visibility">
    Choose **Workspace** (discoverable by all members) or **Invite-only** (restricted to people you explicitly invite). Use invite-only when content must not be discoverable by everyone in the org directory.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign an owner">
    Assign an owner who can approve membership changes. Projects without owners tend to accumulate stale access lists over time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply a template (optional)">
    Start from a blank project or apply a template with prebuilt sections for your team's process. Templates accelerate rollout but still need a quick review—remove sample text before sharing with your team.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Set default labels and owners

Configure default labels and auto-assignment rules so new items do not land unassigned. Defaults reduce triage work at the start of each week when many items are created simultaneously.

<Tip>
Revisit defaults after major process changes—a new severity model or regional expansion. Old defaults silently mis-route work if nobody updates them after a reorg.
</Tip>

## Use templates effectively

When you customize a project template heavily, save a new template version so other teams inherit your improvements instead of editing one-off copies independently.
