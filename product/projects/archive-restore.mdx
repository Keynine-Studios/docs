Archiving a Key Nine Studios project is the gentle off-ramp for completed initiatives: removed from default lists, but fully recoverable when a customer reopens a contract or an audit requires historical context. Plan your data exports before archiving if compliance requires cold storage outside the platform.

## Archive a project

Archiving hides a project from default sidebar lists while preserving all history. Automation associated with the project pauses so you do not accidentally enqueue jobs against dormant data.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open project settings">
    Navigate to the project and open **Settings** from the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Archive the project">
    Click **Archive project** and confirm. The project moves out of your default lists immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Tell stakeholders when you archive a project so they do not assume content was deleted. If work continued elsewhere, link to the new canonical project in a final comment or announcement.
</Note>

### Archive partial work

If only one stream within a project is complete, consider using labels or subfolders instead of archiving the whole project while other tracks remain active.

## Restore a project

Workspace admins can restore archived projects within the retention window defined by your plan. Older archives may require support intervention if your data policy moved content to long-term storage.

After restoring, spot-check permissions and integrations—tokens may have rotated while the project was idle.

## Export before archiving

Export project content before archiving if your compliance policy requires long-term cold storage outside Key Nine Studios. Keep checksums or manifest files so your legal team can prove what was frozen at a specific point in time.
