## Whole Steps \+ Half Steps

A **half step** is represented by the letter "h" in musical formulas and delineates 1 semitone in Western Music. An example of a half step on the keyboard is **E to F** or **B to C**.

A **whole step** is represented by the letter "w" in musical formulas and delineates 2 semitones in Western Music. An example of a whole step on the keyboard is **C to D** or **F to G**

## Note & Rest Values

The values of a note or rest create a ratio with one another, please view the table below:

| Value | Note | Rest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Whole | Whole |
| 1/2 | Half | Half |
| 1/4 | Quarter | Quarter |
| 1/8 | Eighth | Eighth |
| 1/16 | Sixteenth | Sixteenth |

The actual value of each of these depends on [Time Signatures](https://getsupport.keyninestudios.com/music-theory-handbook#time-signatures). Please review that section of the handbook to learn more about them!

## Time Signatures

Time signatures appear as a fraction at the beginning of a line or piece of music, examples include 4/4, 3/4, 5/8, 2/4, 12/8.

The top number in the fraction represents how many beats are in each measure, for example in **4**/4 there are **4** beats per measure.

The bottom number in the fraction represents which note value gets one beat, for example in 12/**8** an **eighth** note would get a beat. 

**Example:** In the time signature of 5/4, there are 5 beats per measure, and the quarter note gets one beat. In other words, there are 5 quarter notes per measure.

## Clef Reading

## Musical Formulas

Musical formulas are a great way to construct any scale! Use the formulas below to help guide you through constructing. Please review [Whole Steps and Half Steps](https://getsupport.keyninestudios.com/music-theory-handbook#whole-steps-%2B-half-steps) in order to understand what the formulas mean.

**Major Scale:** W-W-H-W-W-W-H

**Natural Minor Scale/Aeolian:** W-H-W-W-H-W-W

## Pentatonic & Blues Scales

Unlike the major and minor scales that have 7 notes, the pentatonic and blues scales have 5 and 6 notes respectively. To construct these scales,  see the information below!

**Pentatonic Scale:** To construct the pentatonic scale, simply use the 1 - 2 - 3 - 5 - 6 scale degrees. 

**Blues Scale:** To construct the blues scale, simply use the 1 - 3b - 4 - 5b - 5 - 7 scale degrees.

To learn more about scale degrees, please visit the handbook section, [scale degrees](https://getsupport.keyninestudios.com/music-theory-handbook#scale-degrees).

## Scale Degrees

Every scales has numbers associated with each note of the scale, this allows musicians to talk about concepts that can transfer to any scale instead of just one. The root note of the scale is always the first scale degree. Look at the table below for an example.

| Scale Letter | C | D | E | F | G | A | B | C |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Degrees | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 |
| Name | Tonic | Supertonic | Mediant | Subdominant | Dominant | Submediant | Leading Tone/Subtonic | Tonic |

## Relative Major and Minor Scales

Every major scale has a relative minor scale that has the same key signature, you can get the relative minor from the 6th scale degree of the major scale, to learn more about scale degrees, visit our handbook section on [Scale Degrees](https://getsupport.keyninestudios.com/music-theory-handbook#scale-degrees). 

For example, the relative minor of C major would be A minor.

| C | D | E | F | G | A |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | **6** |

## Modes

The typical major scale is considered a parent scale, from each parent scale you can derive countless other scales starting on each of the scale degrees, to learn more about scale degrees, visit our handbook section [Scale Degrees](https://getsupport.keyninestudios.com/music-theory-handbook#scale-degrees).

| Mode | Starting Scale Degree | Formula |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ionian | 1 | WWHWWWH |
| Dorian | 2 | WHWWWHW |
| Phrygian | 3 | HWWWHWW |
| Lydian | 4 | WWWHWWH |
| Mixolydian | 5 | WWHWWHW |
| Aeolian | 6 | WHWWHWW |
| Locrian | 7 | HWWHWWW |
