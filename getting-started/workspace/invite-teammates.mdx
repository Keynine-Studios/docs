Invitations are the front door to your Key Nine Studios workspace. Clear role assignments and timely follow-up prevent "I never got access" threads during launches. Treat pending invites like a short-lived inventory—review them weekly until everyone has joined.

## Send invites

Navigate to **Settings → Members** and enter one or more email addresses. Assign a default role for each person before sending. Invites expire after fourteen days; resend them if recipients miss the window.

<Tip>
Tell people to expect an invite from a specific sender name so corporate email filters do not hide it. Link to your internal wiki for context on why you adopted Key Nine Studios.
</Tip>

### Bulk import

If your plan supports CSV import, validate your file against HR data first. Typos in email addresses generate unnecessary support tickets and delay access.

## Assign roles at invite time

You can assign **Member**, **Admin**, or custom roles (on supported plans) when you send an invite. You can change roles any time after someone joins.

<Note>
Default to Member unless someone genuinely needs billing or security settings. Elevate admins in pairs so coverage exists during vacation or PTO.
</Note>

## Manage pending invites

Pending invites appear in **Settings → Members → Pending**. You can resend or revoke individual invites from this view.

### Revoke an invite

Revoking a pending invite invalidates the link immediately. The recipient is not notified—the invite link simply stops working. If you revoke because someone left the company, note the reason so future access reviews are accurate.
