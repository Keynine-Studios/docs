Creating your Key Nine Studios account takes only a few minutes. The decisions that matter most—company vs personal workspace, who owns billing, and which email domain controls the org—are much easier to correct now than after you have invited dozens of people.

## Sign up

Visit the signup page and enter your work email. Key Nine Studios sends a one-time verification link to confirm ownership of the address. Links expire after a short window, so request a fresh one if your inbox is slow to receive it.

<Warning>
Avoid signing up with distribution lists or shared inboxes. If nobody owns the mailbox individually, you lose the ability to recover the account later. Use a named admin who can delegate access afterward.
</Warning>

### Domain and IT policy

Some companies block consumer email providers or external notification senders. If signup fails silently, try your corporate domain or ask IT to allowlist the Key Nine Studios notification sender.

## Choose an organization type

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Company">
    Multiple teams under one billing profile. Choose this when finance expects a single invoice and you need shared administration across departments.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Personal">
    Individual use with the option to upgrade later. Suitable for evaluation before you commit to a team rollout or legal entity.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Switching later

Moving between organization types may require support assistance or an export/import if billing identities are separated at the product level. When in doubt, start as **Company** if you know you will onboard a team within a month.

## Troubleshoot verification

If the verification email does not arrive within a few minutes:

1. Check your spam or quarantine folder.
2. Confirm IT allowlists the Key Nine Studios notification domain.
3. Request a new verification link from the signup page.

Corporate filters sometimes delay the first message from a new sender more than subsequent ones.
