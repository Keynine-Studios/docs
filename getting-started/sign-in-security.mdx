Good security in Key Nine Studios balances friction for attackers without blocking legitimate work. Understanding how people authenticate, how long sessions last, and what admins can require before high-risk actions gives you the right foundation before you onboard a full team.

## Authentication options

Key Nine Studios supports multiple authentication methods depending on your plan:

- **Email and password** with optional multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- **SAML SSO** on Team and Enterprise plans
- **IdP-initiated login** where your identity provider supports it

### Set up MFA

Require MFA for every member when your data classification demands it—not only admins. Attackers frequently target contributors with broad file access, not just administrative accounts.

<Tip>
Enable MFA for all workspace members before inviting external contractors or partners.
</Tip>

### Enable SAML SSO

<Steps>
  <Step title="Plan a maintenance window">
    Communicate expected downtime to your team before enabling SAML. Keep a break-glass local admin account active during the transition.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your IdP">
    Add Key Nine Studios as a service provider in your identity provider. Copy the metadata URL or XML from **Settings → Security → SSO**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validate with a pilot group">
    Test attribute mapping (email, name, role) with a small group before enforcing SSO for all members.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enforce SSO">
    Once the pilot succeeds, enable enforcement so all members must use your IdP to sign in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Session management

Sessions expire after a period of inactivity. Admins can require re-authentication for sensitive actions like billing changes or permission updates.

| Session setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Short sessions | Shared or lab machines |
| Longer sessions | Trusted personal laptops |
| Re-auth for sensitive actions | Always enable for admin actions |

### Device hygiene

Encourage disk encryption and screen locks on any machine that holds active session cookies. Remote wipe policies complement session timeouts when hardware is lost or stolen.

## Security best practices

- Enforce MFA for all admins immediately.
- Use separate staging and production workspaces.
- Review connected OAuth apps quarterly and revoke tokens for tools you no longer use.
- Pair OAuth app reviews with offboarding checklists when people change roles.
