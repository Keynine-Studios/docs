The Key Nine Studios Slack integration puts updates where your team already works. A disciplined channel strategy keeps signal high—one noisy channel quickly trains everyone to mute the integration entirely.

## Install the Slack app

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Integrations settings">
    Navigate to **Integrations → Slack** in your Key Nine Studios workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize your Slack workspace">
    Click **Connect Slack** and authorize with the Slack workspace your team uses every day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review OAuth scopes">
    Review the requested permissions during install. If any scope looks broader than your needs, ask your Slack admin whether a reduced configuration is available.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
You need Slack admin approval if your Slack organization restricts third-party app installs. Coordinate with your Slack admin before starting the connection.
</Note>

## Map events to channels

After connecting, select which events post to which channels. Start with a low-traffic channel during your pilot so you can validate the signal before expanding.

<Tip>
Use a consistent prefix for related channels (`#k9-updates-`, `#k9-alerts-`) so people can browse related channels and archive pilot channels after go-live.
</Tip>

## Troubleshoot Slack notifications

If notifications stop arriving:

1. Reauthorize the Slack app from **Integrations → Slack → Reconnect**.
2. Confirm the Key Nine Studios bot was not removed from the target channel.
3. Check integration logs for `429` rate limit responses if you have high event volume—batch summaries or increase the notification interval.
