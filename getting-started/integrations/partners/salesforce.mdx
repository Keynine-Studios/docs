The Key Nine Studios Salesforce connector reduces copy-paste between your CRM and your workspace. Plan your object mapping before syncing thousands of rows—rework is cheaper in a spreadsheet than in production data.

## Prerequisites

- Admin access in your Salesforce org
- An administrator in Key Nine Studios to approve the connector installation

<Tip>
Pilot against a full or partial Salesforce sandbox before connecting to production. Validate picklist values, required fields, and validation rules that might block writes from Key Nine Studios.
</Tip>

## Connect Salesforce

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Integrations">
    Navigate to **Integrations → Partner connectors → Salesforce**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authenticate with Salesforce">
    Click **Connect** and authorize with a Salesforce account that has API access. A service account is recommended for production.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map objects">
    Map Salesforce accounts, contacts, and opportunities to the corresponding entities in Key Nine Studios. Resolve duplicates by external ID where possible.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a sync mode">
    Select one-way (Salesforce → Key Nine Studios) or two-way sync depending on which system owns each field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate and monitor">
    Activate the connector and monitor the first sync. Set up alerts for sync lag and error rates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Define record ownership

Decide who owns records when Salesforce and Key Nine Studios disagree on a field value. Document the rule in your team runbook so support can answer "why did this flip?" without digging through logs.

<Warning>
Monitor for poison records—individual Salesforce records that fail validation on every sync attempt and retry forever. Filter them out or fix the underlying data issue to keep your sync healthy.
</Warning>
