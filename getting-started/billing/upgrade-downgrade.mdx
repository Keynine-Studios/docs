Changing your Key Nine Studios plan affects billing, feature availability, and in some cases data retention. Read through the relevant section before making a change mid-cycle—especially for downgrades that reduce automation limits or storage.

## Upgrade your plan

Upgrades take effect immediately. When you upgrade mid-cycle, proration is calculated against your current billing period so you only pay for the remaining days.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Billing settings">
    Navigate to **Settings → Billing** and click **Change plan**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the new plan">
    Review the feature comparison and confirm your selection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm payment">
    Review the prorated amount and confirm. Your new limits take effect immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### After upgrading

After upgrading, revisit your automation and storage settings. Limits that were previously capped may now be configurable, and you may need to raise internal quotas to take advantage of the new plan.

<Note>
Tell finance when upgrades happen mid-cycle so invoices match purchase orders. Link to the order confirmation in your internal ticket or Slack thread.
</Note>

## Downgrade your plan

Downgrades apply at the start of your next billing cycle unless your contract states otherwise. Features above the new plan's limits become read-only until you reduce your usage below the cap.

<Warning>
Download critical exports before downgrade if your current plan offers longer data retention windows than the lower tier. Grace periods for compliance archives vary—do not rely on them without confirming in advance.
</Warning>

## Keep billing contacts current

Ensure finance and IT stakeholders are copied on invoices if your organization requires it. Rotate billing contacts when the account owner changes roles—stale addresses delay tax validation and payment matching.
