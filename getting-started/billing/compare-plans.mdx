Key Nine Studios plans bundle different combinations of member limits, automation volume, support tiers, and compliance features. Use this page to shortlist your options before legal review—exact numbers may vary, but the pattern holds: you pay for seats, automation volume, and the audit artifacts your organization requires.

## Plan overview

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Free">
    Core projects and limited automation runs. Best for evaluation and small teams with light automation needs. No credit card required.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Team">
    SSO options, higher automation limits, and priority email support. The common step after product-market fit when coordination overhead grows across multiple teams.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Enterprise">
    Regional data residency options, advanced audit logging, and dedicated support channels. Typical when procurement, security questionnaires, or compliance requirements enter the conversation.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Choose the right plan

Start where your current limits hurt most—usually automation volume or member count—then upgrade to Enterprise when procurement or compliance requirements demand it.

<Tip>
Before upgrading, reproduce one painful workflow in a trial project and measure time saved. Finance teams respond to minutes per week reclaimed, not feature lists alone.
</Tip>

### Watch for hidden costs

Count integrations, API call volume, and retained history—not just seats. A team that looks small on paper may exceed automation quotas during a busy quarter or peak launch period.

## Pilot checklist

Before signing a plan:

- Confirm how many seats you truly need after removing inactive accounts.
- List must-have integrations and confirm they are included or priced as add-ons.
- Align legal and security teams on the DPA, subprocessors list, and data residency before you sign.

## When to revisit your plan

Re-evaluate at major milestones: funding rounds, geographic expansion, or a spike in automation usage after a new workflow goes live. Annual true-ups catch drift between contract terms and actual usage.
