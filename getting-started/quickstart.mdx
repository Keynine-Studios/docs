This guide gives a small team a fast path from zero to a working pilot: verified accounts, a safe collaboration space, and one integration proving data can flow in and out. Adjust the order if your security team requires SSO before you send invites.

## Before you begin

- A verified work email address you can access
- Admin access if you plan to enable SSO
- A rough idea of who belongs in the pilot group (five to fifteen people is typical)

<Tip>
If you are migrating from another tool, keep a CSV of users and their roles handy—you will move faster than recreating access from memory.
</Tip>

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your organization">
    Pick a name your team will recognize in notifications. Avoid internal codenames if customers might see the name on shared artifacts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite teammates">
    Start with a small pilot group before rolling out widely. Ask them to accept within a week so you are not debugging expired invites during launch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect one integration">
    Choose Slack or your CRM to see data flow end to end. Validate credentials in a sandbox or test channel first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your first project">
    Use it as a sandbox for workflows and permissions. Seed a few realistic tasks so demos feel grounded.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
If any step is blocked by IT (DNS, SSO metadata, firewall rules), parallelize what you can—draft project structure while tickets move.
</Note>
